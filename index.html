Introduccion

I am a tenure-track investigator at the Design Institute for Automated Manufacturing and Production (IDF) of the Polytechnic University of Valencia (UPV) studying the physical properties of advanced material under extreme conditions. I am a physicist with holds a PhD in Physics giving an answer to the electronic structure of ZnO-based wide band gap semiconductors. He has led several researches (25 financed proposals in large facilities) at ESRF (European Synchrotron Radiation Facility) or ALBA (synchrotrons) and was hired as a responsible for the Fourier-transform Infrared (FTIR) facility belonging to the MALTA-Consolider Ingenio 2010 (5 million €). I have worked through stays and collaborations at different universities and research institutions in Spain, France and Germany. I got a Juan de la Cierva fellow (2012-2015) at IDF (UPV). Since 2016 (-2021) I am a Ramón y Cajal Fellow, a highly competitive and prestigious national research contract, which aims to consolidate researchers with extraordinary potential. Thank to this I already coordinate and supervises (supervision of graduate students and postdoctoral fellows) two PhD and three Degree students, participating in 5 thesis evaluation panels and taking part of 80 contributions to international and national conferences. My research combines state-of-the-art methods to answer to the behaviour of inorganic materials under extreme conditions. Leading the national project InHeXtrem. Several highly competitive awards as The Group Achievement Awards 2013 granted by NASA have recognized my skills and research contributions. My h-index is 19 (22 google scholar).
